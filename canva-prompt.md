# Canva Code prompt (secondary deliverable)

Paste the prompt below into Canva Code to get a second take on the same landing page. It is written
to match the direction we built, so you can compare approaches side by side.

Tip: after it generates, ask Canva Code to "make the hero headline bigger and add a yellow highlight
behind the last two words", or "add an auto-rotating Instagram-style carousel", to push it further.

## Prompt

Build a single-page, mobile-first, high-conversion landing page for **ReThinkWelly**, a
community-led urban-futures project in Wellington, New Zealand. It takes real forgotten city
spaces (empty lots, tired carparks, dead streets) and uses AI to render bold before/after
visions of what they could become. The whole page has one goal: get people to **submit a space**.

**Brand:** bold, optimistic, community-led, no jargon, Wellingtonian, with te reo Maori woven
in naturally (Te Whanganui-a-Tara for Wellington, Kia ora, "join the waka", Aotearoa). Colours:
warm brand yellow (#FEE05A), near-black (#121110), white. The wordmark is a serif + sans lockup:
"ReThink" in a high-contrast serif (the "Re" in italic) followed by "Welly." in a bold sans, with
a trailing full stop, often inside a yellow circle. Use that same move (italic serif accent words
dropped into bold sans headlines) as the signature. The images do the heavy lifting. Do not use em
dashes anywhere in the copy.

**Tagline:** "Dream a little." Hashtag: #ReThinkWelly. Mark: "Since 2026."

**Sections, top to bottom:**

1. **Sticky header** with the wordmark "ReThinkwelly" (the "welly" part highlighted in yellow),
   small links to Instagram / LinkedIn / email, and one yellow "Submit a space" button.
2. **Hero** on a dark background with a faint city-skyline image. Huge headline: "What if
   Wellington looked a little different?" with a yellow highlight sweeping behind "little
   different?". Subtext: "Not a little tidier. Not a little safer. A little bolder. A little more
   alive. We take the spaces our city has forgotten about and show what they could become."
   Two buttons: "Submit a space" (yellow) and "See the vision" (outline). Show the tagline
   "Dream a little. #ReThinkWelly" and a row of Instagram / LinkedIn / email icons.
3. **Mission + proof:** a short bold statement about Wellington being one of the most loveable
   cities but honest about its empty gaps and car-first streets, next to three animated stat
   counters: "27 spaces reimagined", "140+ ideas submitted", "4,200+ community following".
4. **How it works:** heading "Real spaces. Bold ideas. No permission needed." and three steps:
   1) Spot a space, 2) Render the future with AI, 3) Share it out loud.
5. **Before/after gallery (the centrepiece):** interactive draggable before/after image sliders
   with a yellow drag handle and location labels for the three real projects: "134 Courtenay Place",
   "Hnry Stadium" (a roof over the Cake Tin) and "Left Bank, Cuba Street". Make this the largest,
   most confident section.
6. **Instagram:** a dark section titled "See it as it happens" with an auto-rotating carousel of
   recent posts and a yellow "@rethinkwelly" button linking to the Instagram profile.
7. **Submit a space (the conversion goal):** a bold yellow section with the headline "See a space
   Wellington is wasting?", a short form with only three fields plus email (Where is it? / What is
   there now? / Your email) and a "Send it in" button. Include a line: "Get involved as a Creative,
   Business, Developer or Local."
8. **Footer** on a dark background: large "ReThinkwelly" wordmark, the tagline "Stop accepting
   Wellington as it is. Start imagining what it could be. Dream a little.", large Instagram /
   LinkedIn / email links, and rethinkwelly.com.

**Interactions:** subtle scroll-reveal on sections, animated number counters, buttery before/after
sliders, an auto-rotating Instagram carousel that pauses on hover, and a sticky "Submit a space"
button on mobile. All motion must respect reduced-motion settings. Keep it fast and lightweight.

**Conversion rules to follow:** one clear call to action repeated down the page, a benefit-led
hero headline, social proof high up, a very short form, and no distracting navigation menu.
